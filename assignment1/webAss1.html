Home Affairs

Home Affairs brings together Australia's federal law enforcement, national and transport security, criminal justice, emergency management citizenship and border control.





What We DO

The Department is responsible for central coordination, and strategy and policy leadership in relation to: cyber and critical infrastructure resilience and security. immigration.





The Department of Home Affairs in Australia is a key government agency responsible for various aspects of national security, immigration, border control, and law enforcement. It plays a central role in managing and protecting Australia's borders, citizens, and residents.

The Department of Home Affairs plays a crucial role in safeguarding Australia's national interests, ensuring border security, managing immigration,

and contributing to the nation's overall security and well-being. It is a multifaceted organization with diverse
responsibilities and is a key component of the Australian government's efforts to protect its citizens and maintain the integrity of its borders.
